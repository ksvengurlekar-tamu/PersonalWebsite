Volunteering

Throughout high school, I participated in a service outreach organization called the Young Men's Service League. I held the role of president of that organization, while simultaneously doing 150 hours of service. I volunteered with organizations such as Rainbow Days, The Dallas Ramp Project, Spokes for Folks, and Feed my Starving Children. Pictured here is me after a food packing session for Feed my Starving Children. In college, I participated in a leadership organization called Leaders Advancing in Service and Honor. This organization held service events and donation drives for the local communnity and philanthropies such as Twin City Missions.

Fish Camp Counselor

I participated in one of Texas A&M's first tradition: Fish Camp. I was a counselor for fifteen incoming freshmen during the time of camp and continued to be a mentor throughout their first year on their own in college. The main focus of camp is to accustom the freshmen to life on campus and demonstrate what it means to be an Aggie at Texas A&M. During camp, I led group discussions, conducted bonding activities, and gave guidance about the social norms of college life at A&M. While working with a team of thirty other counselors, I hosted and delegated a group of 150 incoming freshmen over the course of the camp.
